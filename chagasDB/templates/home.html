Welcome to ChagasDB!

Chagas disease is a parasitical disease caused by Trypanosoma cruzi, a protozoan endemic from south America. This parasite can infect many mammals, like domestic dogs, raccoons, or humans. It is mostly asymptomatic, but it can lead to several complications in 30-40% of infected patients, notably cardiac, but also digestive or nervous.

ChagasDB is a manual-curated database regrouping published results referenced in Pubmed. For now, it gathers all the molecules significantly associated with T.cruzi infection in different host species, including genes, proteins, polymorphisms, hormones, or other chemical compounds. Facilitating access to all this information is intended to improve the understanding of chagas disease, and thus the living conditions of those affected. Simplified access to all this information is intended to improve the understanding of chagas disease, and thus facilitate the search for a treatment.
